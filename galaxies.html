What is a Nebula?

In Short

Nebulae are vast clouds of gas and dust in space. Some are stellar nurseries where stars are born; others are the glowing remains of dying stars.

Types

  • Emission: glow red from ionized hydrogen (H‚Äëalpha).
  • Reflection: appear blue from scattered starlight.
  • Dark: dense dust blocks background light.
  • Planetary: shells from Sun‚Äëlike dying stars.
  • Supernova remnants: expanding debris from stellar explosions.

Scale, Color & Observing

  • Scale: can span many light‚Äëyears (Orion ~24 ly wide).
  • Why colorful? Long exposures map faint light; colors often represent different gases.
  • Observation tips: seek dark skies; use UHC/OIII filters for emission nebulae.

How to Observe

Use dark skies and a star chart or app. A small telescope reveals more structure; long-exposure images show even more detail.
