Famous Comets

Icy visitors that grow glowing tails when they heat near the Sun.

1P/Halley Hale–Bopp NEOWISE 67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko 2P/Encke

1P/Halley

Periodic • ~76‑Year Return
The most famous periodic comet, last seen in 1986 and due again in 2061.
  • Source of the Eta Aquariids and Orionids meteor showers.
  • Observed for millennia; predicted by Edmond Halley.

Comet Hale–Bopp (C/1995 O1)

Great Comet • Extremely Bright
A spectacular naked‑eye comet in 1997, visible for a record 18 months.
  • Large nucleus and high activity made it prominent worldwide.
  • One of the most observed comets of the 20th century.

Comet NEOWISE (C/2020 F3)

Recent Visitor • Twin Tails
A bright 2020 comet visible from both hemispheres with dust and ion tails.
  • Period ~6,800 years; discovered by NASA’s NEOWISE mission.
  • Produced photogenic twilight views during northern summer.

67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko

Rosetta Mission • Bilobed Nucleus
Target of ESA’s Rosetta spacecraft, which orbited and deployed the Philae lander.
  • Peanut‑shaped nucleus; jets and surface changes observed up close.
  • Period ~6.5 years; source of detailed comet science.

2P/Encke

Shortest Known Period • ~3.3 Years
A frequently returning comet with a very short orbital period.
  • Associated with the Taurid meteor stream.
  • Likely a fragment of a much larger ancient comet.

Black Holes

Regions where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape.

Sagittarius A* M87* Cygnus X‑1 V404 Cygni Merging Black Holes

Sagittarius A*

Supermassive • Milky Way Center
A ~4 million‑solar‑mass black hole at the heart of our galaxy, imaged by the Event Horizon Telescope.
  • Its gravity steers stars in tight orbits near the galactic center.
  • Radio bright; variable on hour‑long timescales.

M87*

First BH Image • Powerful Jet
The first black hole ever imaged (2019), in galaxy M87; launches a relativistic jet thousands of light‑years long.
  • Mass ~6.5 billion Suns; distance ~55 million light‑years.
  • Shadow size matched general relativity predictions.

Cygnus X‑1

Stellar‑Mass • X‑ray Binary
One of the first strong black hole candidates, siphoning matter from a massive companion star and emitting X‑rays.
  • Mass ~20 Suns; distance ~7,200 light‑years.
  • Accretion disk and jets produce variable X‑ray signals.

V404 Cygni

Stellar‑Mass • Outbursts
A black hole binary known for dramatic flares as it devours material from its companion.
  • Shows rapid, repeated outbursts observed across wavelengths.
  • Evidence of precessing jets in some episodes.

Merging Black Holes

Gravitational Waves • LIGO/Virgo
Pairs of black holes spiral together and merge, sending ripples in spacetime detected as gravitational waves.
  • First detection (GW150914) announced in 2016.
  • Reveals populations of heavy stellar black holes.
